From limited IT training to studying in the open.

I didn't come up through a CS degree or a help-desk job. I started with curiosity, a stack of CompTIA objectives, and a decision to document the whole climb instead of waiting until I'd "made it."

Why a public study log

Writing is how I actually learn — if I can explain subnetting clearly enough to publish, I understand it. Doing it in public adds accountability and, over time, builds the thing a career-changer needs most: visible, verifiable proof of work. Every post is something I learned; every project is something I built and can defend in an interview.

The plan

Three certs, in order: A+ for fundamentals, Network+ for the wire, Security+ for the career goal. Alongside the reading, a home lab where the concepts get real — scanners, SIEMs, firewalls, and the occasional thing I break and have to fix.

If you're on the same road, I hope the honest version — including the slow weeks — is more useful than a highlight reel.
