Vulnerability Management in My Home Lab: 47 Hosts, 12 Criticals → 3

I built a credentialed-scan pipeline with OpenVAS, triaged by CVSS, actually remediated, and re-scanned. Here's the report — and what broke along the way.

#The goal

I wanted a real vulnerability-management workflow — not a one-off scan, but the full loop: discover, scan with credentials, triage by severity, remediate, and prove it with a re-scan. The deliverable was a report I'd be comfortable handing an interviewer.

#Lab setup & the scan

Greenbone/OpenVAS on a management VM, with Metasploitable and a Windows 10 eval box as deliberately-vulnerable targets, all on an isolated VirtualBox network. Credentialed scanning is the part people skip — and it's where the real findings live.

bash · scan + triage 
# credentialed scan, full-and-fast against the lab subnet
gvm-cli socket --xml \
  "<create_task>...<config id='full-and-fast'/>...</create_task>"

# triage: keep only CVSS >= 9.0, group by host
./triage.py --min-cvss 9.0 --group-by host results.xml

#Triage & results

First scan: 47 hosts, an ugly pile of findings. I triaged by CVSS, fixed the criticals and most highs, then re-scanned to prove the deltas instead of claiming them.

#What broke

Lesson: credentialed scans failed silently on 6 Windows hosts — WinRM wasn't enabled, so they came back looking clean. Always validate authentication succeeded before you trust a "no findings" result.

Once WinRM was fixed and re-scanned, those "clean" hosts surfaced four of the remaining criticals. Proof-of-work, including the full before/after report and the triage script, is in the repo.
